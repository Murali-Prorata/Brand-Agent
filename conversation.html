Perfect! One more thing - any tips for keeping the kids engaged at historical sites? I worry they might get bored.

ü§ñ

Excellent concern! Here are proven strategies to keep kids engaged at historical sites:

Before You Go

  • Watch kid-friendly documentaries about Rome/gladiators
  • Read books like "Magic Tree House: Vacation Under the Volcano"
  • Show them pictures and get them excited about specific things

At the Sites

  • Scavenger hunts: Create lists of things to find (specific columns, animals in mosaics, etc.)
  • Story-telling: Make history come alive with dramatic retellings
  • Photography missions: Let them be "official trip photographers"
  • Sketching: Bring notebooks to draw what they see
  • Guided tours for kids: Many attractions offer family-specific tours

Timing Strategies

  • Visit major sites early morning when kids are fresh
  • Limit to 1-2 big attractions per day
  • Alternate museum days with outdoor/fun activities
  • Build in playground time at parks

Rewards System

Consider a small reward system - after visiting a historical site, they get to choose the next gelato stop or activity. This gives them something to look forward to!
